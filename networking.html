Networking

Connecting the World

Networking is the backbone of modern communication, allowing devices to share information and resources. It encompasses a wide range of technologies and protocols that facilitate the movement of data across systems.

Types of Networks

  • LAN (Local Area Network) – A network that connects devices within a limited area such as an office or home.
  • WAN (Wide Area Network) – A network that spans large geographical areas, like cities or countries.
  • MAN (Metropolitan Area Network) – A network that covers a larger area than a LAN but smaller than a WAN, often used in cities.
  • VPN (Virtual Private Network) – A secure, encrypted network connection over the internet.

Networking Protocols

  • TCP/IP – The fundamental protocol for internet and network communication.
  • HTTP/HTTPS – Protocols for transmitting data on the web.
  • FTP – A protocol used to transfer files over a network.
  • DNS – A system that translates domain names into IP addresses.

Careers in Networking

Networking professionals play a crucial role in maintaining the infrastructure of connected systems. Key roles include:

  • Network Engineer – Designs, implements, and manages networks.
  • Network Administrator – Maintains network hardware and software.
  • Network Security Specialist – Focuses on securing networks from threats.
